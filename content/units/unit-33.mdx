## Learning Goal
Use **should**, **shouldn't**, and **should have done** to give advice, judge decisions, and talk about a better past choice.

## Core Grammar
**Should + base verb** gives advice now. **Should have + past participle** means the better action was not done in the past.

## Examples
- You should review the examples before doing the exercise.
- You shouldn't translate every sentence word by word.
- I should have checked the tense before answering.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- **Should have done** is about the past, not the future.
- **Shouldn't have done** often sounds like criticism.

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: I should checked the answer.
- Correct: I should have checked the answer.
- Wrong: You should to practice more.
- Correct: You should practice more.

## One-line Summary
Use **should** for advice now and **should have done** for advice about the past.
