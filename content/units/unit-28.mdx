title: must and can't

## Learning Goal
EN: Use **must** and **can't** for logical deduction.

中：學會用 **must** 和 **can't** 表達「我推論一定是 / 一定不是」。

## Core Grammar
### A. Must / 一定是
EN: Use **must** when you are almost sure something is true.

中：你幾乎確定某件事是真的，用 **must**。

- She must be tired.
- This must be the correct pattern.

### B. Can't / 一定不是
EN: Use **can't** when you are almost sure something is not true.

中：你幾乎確定某件事不可能是真的，用 **can't**。

- This answer can't be correct.
- He can't be at home. The lights are off.

### C. Past Deduction / 對過去推論
EN: Use **must have done** or **can't have done** for deduction about the past.

中：對過去推論，用 **must have done** 或 **can't have done**。

- He must have misunderstood the question.
- She can't have finished already.

### D. Mustn't Is Different / mustn't 不一樣
EN: **Mustn't** means prohibition. It does not mean negative deduction.

中：**mustn't** 是禁止，不是「一定不是」。負面推論用 **can't**。

- You mustn't smoke here. = prohibited
- She can't be there. = negative deduction

## Examples
- This sentence must be passive.
- That answer can't be right.
- I must have chosen the wrong tense.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- EN: **Mustn't** is not negative deduction. It means prohibition.
- 中：**mustn't** 不是「一定不是」，而是「禁止」。
- EN: Negative deduction uses **can't**.
- 中：負面推論用 **can't**。

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: She mustn't be at home. The lights are off.
- Correct: She can't be at home. The lights are off.
- Wrong: He must has forgotten.
- Correct: He must have forgotten.

## One-line Summary
EN: **Must** = almost sure yes. **Can't** = almost sure no.

中：**must** = 幾乎確定是；**can't** = 幾乎確定不是。
