## Learning Goal
Use **should have done** and **shouldn't have done** to talk about better choices in the past.

## Core Grammar
**Should have + past participle** means the better action was not done. **Shouldn't have + past participle** means the action happened, but it was not a good idea.

## Examples
- I should have checked the tense before answering.
- You should have used the passive form here.
- I shouldn't have rushed through the review test.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- **Should have done** is about the past, not the future.
- It often sounds like regret, criticism, or correction.

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: I should checked the answer.
- Correct: I should have checked the answer.
- Wrong: You shouldn't did that.
- Correct: You shouldn't have done that.

## One-line Summary
Use **should have done** when the better choice was in the past.
