## Learning Goal
Use **wish** patterns for present regrets, past regrets, and complaints about repeated behavior.

## Core Grammar
Use **wish + past** for unreal present, **wish + past perfect** for past regret, and **wish + would** when you want someone or something to change.

## Examples
- I wish I understood this rule better.
- I wish I had noticed the passive form earlier.
- I wish I wouldn't rush when I answer grammar questions.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- **Wish** is different from **hope**. **Hope** is for possible future outcomes.
- **Wish + would** usually expresses annoyance or desire for change.

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: I wish I will improve soon.
- Better: I hope I will improve soon.
- Wrong: I wish I studied yesterday.
- Better: I wish I had studied yesterday.

## One-line Summary
Use **wish** for unreal or regretful situations, and **hope** for possible future outcomes.
