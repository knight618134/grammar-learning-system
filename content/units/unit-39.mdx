## Learning Goal
Use **if + past** and **wish + past** for unreal present situations.

## Core Grammar
Use the past form to talk about an unreal present or future: **If I knew...**, **I wish I knew...**. The meaning is present, even though the verb form is past.

## Examples
- If I knew the answer, I would explain it.
- I wish I knew why this sentence uses passive.
- If I had more time, I could review Unit 42 again.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- **I wish I had...** can mean "I don't have it now."
- The past form after **wish** often describes an unreal present.

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: I wish I have more time.
- Correct: I wish I had more time.
- Wrong: If I know the answer, I would tell you.
- Correct: If I knew the answer, I would tell you.

## One-line Summary
Use **wish + past** for something you want to be different now.
