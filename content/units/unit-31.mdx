## Learning Goal
EN: Distinguish **have to** and **must** for obligation.

中：分辨 **have to** 和 **must**：都可以表示必須，但語感不完全一樣。

## Core Grammar
### A. Have To / 外在要求
EN: **Have to** often means the obligation comes from outside rules, schedules, or situations.

中：**have to** 常表示外在規定、情況、時間壓力造成的必須。

- I have to finish the review today.
- Students have to submit the test.

### B. Must / 強烈要求或個人判斷
EN: **Must** can be a strong rule, instruction, or personal feeling.

中：**must** 可以是強烈規定，也可以是說話者自己的強烈判斷。

- I must remember this pattern.
- You must check the verb form.

### C. Past Obligation / 過去必須
EN: Use **had to** for past obligation. Do not usually use **must** for past obligation.

中：過去的必須通常用 **had to**。

- I had to review Unit 42 again.

### D. Question And Negative / 問句和否定
EN: **Have to** uses do/does/did in questions and negatives.

中：**have to** 的問句和否定常用 do/does/did。

- Do you have to review this?
- You don't have to finish it today.

## Examples
- I have to review the wrong answers.
- I must stop rushing.
- I had to check the passive form twice.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- EN: **Have to** changes with tense: have to, has to, had to.
- 中：**have to** 會跟時態和主詞變化。
- EN: **Must** does not use **to** after it.
- 中：must 後面直接接原形動詞。

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: I must to study.
- Correct: I must study.
- Wrong: He have to review.
- Correct: He has to review.

## One-line Summary
EN: **Have to** often comes from outside; **must** often feels stronger or more personal.

中：**have to** 常是外在要求；**must** 語氣更強或更主觀。
