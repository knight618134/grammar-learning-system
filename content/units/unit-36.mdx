## Learning Goal
Choose between **can**, **could**, and **be able to** for ability, possibility, and successful action.

## Core Grammar
**Can** describes present ability. **Could** describes general past ability. **Was/were able to** often describes success in one specific past situation.

## Examples
- I can understand the rule now.
- I could read simple English when I was younger.
- I was able to finish the review test yesterday.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- For one successful past action, use **was able to**, not usually **could**.
- **Could** can also make requests more polite.

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: I could finish the test yesterday.
- Better: I was able to finish the test yesterday.
- Wrong: Can you helped me?
- Correct: Can you help me?

## One-line Summary
Use **could** for general past ability and **was able to** for a specific success.
