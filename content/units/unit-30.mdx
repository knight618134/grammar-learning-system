## Learning Goal
EN: Use **may/might have done** for uncertain past possibilities.

中：用 **may/might have done** 表達「過去可能發生了，但我不確定」。

## Core Grammar
### A. May/Might Have Done / 過去可能
EN: Use **may/might have + past participle** for uncertain past events.

中：用 **may/might have + 過去分詞** 表達對過去的可能推測。

- She might have chosen the wrong tense.
- I may have misunderstood the question.

### B. Not The Same As Could Have / 和 could have 不一樣
EN: **Might have done** focuses on uncertainty. **Could have done** often focuses on past possibility or missed chance.

中：**might have done** 重點是不確定；**could have done** 常常是本來可以或錯過機會。

### C. Form Check / 形式檢查
EN: After **have**, use a past participle.

中：have 後面接過去分詞。

- might have answered
- may have forgotten
- might have been corrected

### D. Passive Possibility / 被動可能性
EN: For passive past possibility, use **may/might have been + past participle**.

中：如果是「過去可能被...」，用 **may/might have been + 過去分詞**。

- The file might have been deleted.
- The sentence may have been corrected already.

## Examples
- I might have saved the wrong answer already.
- The sentence may have been corrected.
- Jason might have confused **been done** with **being done**.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- EN: **Might have done** does not automatically mean regret.
- 中：**might have done** 不一定是後悔，主要是不確定。
- EN: For passive, use **may/might have been done**.
- 中：如果是被動，要用 **may/might have been done**。

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: She might has answered already.
- Correct: She might have answered already.
- Wrong: It might have correct.
- Correct: It might have been corrected.

## One-line Summary
EN: **May/might have done** = uncertain possibility about the past.

中：**may/might have done** = 對過去不確定的推測。
