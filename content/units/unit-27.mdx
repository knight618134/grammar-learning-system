title: could (do) and could have (done)

## Learning Goal
EN: Distinguish **could do** from **could have done**.

中：分辨 **could do** 和 **could have done**：一個是現在/未來可能，一個是過去本來可以。

## Core Grammar
### A. Could Do / 可以做、可能做
EN: **Could + base verb** means something is possible or is a suggestion.

中：**could + 原形動詞** 表示可能性或建議。

- We could review Passive again.
- You could use a simpler sentence.

### B. Could Have Done / 本來可以做
EN: **Could have + past participle** means something was possible in the past, but did not happen or is uncertain.

中：**could have + 過去分詞** 表示過去本來可以，但可能沒做。

- I could have answered correctly, but I rushed.

### C. Jason Warning / Jason 注意
EN: Do not write **could have do** or **could have answer**.

中：have 後面要接過去分詞：**could have done / answered / checked**。

## Examples
- I could review Unit 42 first.
- I could have reviewed Unit 42 before Unit 43.
- This mistake could have been avoided.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- EN: **Could do** is not past regret by itself.
- 中：**could do** 本身不是過去後悔。
- EN: **Could have done** often sounds like missed opportunity.
- 中：**could have done** 常有「當時本來可以」的感覺。

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: I could have answer it.
- Correct: I could have answered it.
- Wrong: I could have do more practice.
- Correct: I could have done more practice.

## One-line Summary
EN: **Could have done** means a past possibility or missed chance.

中：**could have done** = 過去本來可以做。
