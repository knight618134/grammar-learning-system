## Learning Goal
EN: Use **may** and **might** for uncertain present or future possibilities.

中：用 **may / might** 表達「可能，但不確定」。

## Core Grammar
### A. May / Might + Base Verb / 可能會
EN: Use **may/might + base verb** when something is possible but not certain.

中：用 **may/might + 原形動詞** 表達可能性。

- I may review Unit 43 tonight.
- This sentence might be passive.

### B. May vs Might / 差異
EN: **May** and **might** are often similar. **Might** can feel a little less certain.

中：兩者常常很接近；**might** 有時感覺更不確定一點。

### C. Negative Possibility / 可能不
EN: Use **may not / might not** for uncertain negative possibilities.

中：用 **may not / might not** 表達「可能不會 / 可能不是」。

- This answer might not be correct.

## Examples
- I may need more examples.
- It might be easier with Chinese explanation.
- The answer may not be passive.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- EN: **May/might** is uncertainty, not strong deduction.
- 中：**may/might** 是不確定，不是像 must/can't 那麼強。
- EN: Do not add **to** after may/might.
- 中：may/might 後面直接接原形動詞，不加 to。

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: It may to be correct.
- Correct: It may be correct.
- Wrong: I might to review it.
- Correct: I might review it.

## One-line Summary
EN: **May/might** = possible but not certain.

中：**may/might** = 可能，但不確定。
