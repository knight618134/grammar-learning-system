## Learning Goal
EN: Use **have something done** when someone arranges for another person to do a service or action.

中：學會用 **have something done** 表達「請人/安排別人幫你做某事」。

## Core Grammar
### A. Have + Object + Past Participle / 請人做某事
EN: Use **have + object + past participle** when the subject does not do the action personally.

中：當主詞不是自己做，而是請別人或安排別人做，用 **have + 受詞 + 過去分詞**。

- I had my phone repaired.
- Jason had his sentence checked.

### B. Different From Passive / 和一般被動不同
EN: This pattern focuses on arranging a service, not only on receiving an action.

中：這個句型重點是「安排/請人做」，不是單純被動。

- My phone was repaired. = phone is the focus
- I had my phone repaired. = I arranged it

### C. Get Something Done / get 版本
EN: **Get something done** is also common and often more informal.

中：**get something done** 也常見，語氣比較口語。

- I got my notes checked.

### D. Bad Experience / 不好的遭遇
EN: The pattern can also describe something bad that happened to someone.

中：這個句型也可用於不好的遭遇。

- He had his bike stolen.

## Examples
- Jason had his answer checked.
- I had my notes organized.
- We got the wrong answers reviewed.
- He had his phone repaired yesterday.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- EN: The object receives the action.
- 中：中間那個受詞是承受動作的人/物。
- EN: Use past participle after the object.
- 中：受詞後面接過去分詞。

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: I had my sentence check.
- Correct: I had my sentence checked.
- Wrong: Jason had checked his answer. (different meaning)
- Better: Jason had his answer checked.

## One-line Summary
EN: **Have something done** means arranging for something to be done by someone else.

中：**have something done** = 安排/請別人把某事做好。
