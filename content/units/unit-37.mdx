title: Could do / Could have done
---

## Learning Goal
Use **could do** for present or future possibility and **could have done** for a past possibility that did not happen.

## Core Grammar
**Could + base verb** means something is possible. **Could have + past participle** means something was possible in the past, but it may not have happened.

## Examples
- We could review Unit 43 tomorrow.
- You could use a shorter sentence here.
- I could have answered correctly, but I rushed.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- **Could have done** is not the same as **could do**.
- It often carries regret or missed opportunity.

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: I could have answer it.
- Correct: I could have answered it.
- Wrong: Maybe I could have do more practice.
- Correct: Maybe I could have done more practice.

## One-line Summary
Use **could have done** for a possible past action that was missed or uncertain.
