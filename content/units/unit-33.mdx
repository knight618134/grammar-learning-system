## Learning Goal
Use **should** and **shouldn't** for advice, opinions, expected situations, and sensible choices.

## Core Grammar
**Should + base verb** gives advice or says what is the right thing to do. It can also show that something is expected.

## Examples
- You should review the confusing points before doing new exercises.
- This answer should be correct, but check the tense again.
- You shouldn't translate every sentence word by word.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- **Should** is weaker than **must**.
- **Should** can mean advice, but it can also mean expectation.

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: You should to practice more.
- Correct: You should practice more.
- Wrong: This should to be correct.
- Correct: This should be correct.

## One-line Summary
Use **should + base verb** for advice and expected situations.
