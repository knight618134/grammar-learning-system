title: Have to / Must / Should
---

## Learning Goal
Separate obligation, prohibition, lack of obligation, and advice.

## Core Grammar
**Have to** often means external obligation. **Must** can be strong personal obligation or rule. **Mustn't** means prohibition. **Don't have to** means there is no obligation.

## Examples
- I have to finish the review test today.
- You must not use this answer pattern here.
- You don't have to memorize every example.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- **Mustn't** and **don't have to** are very different.
- **Should** is advice, weaker than **must** or **have to**.

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: You don't have to smoke here.
- Correct: You mustn't smoke here.
- Wrong: I must to study.
- Correct: I must study.

## One-line Summary
Use **mustn't** for prohibition and **don't have to** for no obligation.
