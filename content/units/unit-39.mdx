## Learning Goal
Use **may**, **might**, and **may/might have done** to express uncertainty.

## Core Grammar
**May** and **might** show that something is possible but not certain. **May/might have + past participle** talks about uncertain past events.

## Examples
- I may review Unit 42 again tonight.
- This sentence might be passive.
- She might have chosen the wrong tense.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- **Might have done** is about uncertain past events.
- It does not automatically mean regret; context decides.

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: She might has answered already.
- Correct: She might have answered already.
- Wrong: It may to be correct.
- Correct: It may be correct.

## One-line Summary
Use **may/might** for possibility and **may/might have done** for uncertain past possibility.
