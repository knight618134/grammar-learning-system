## Learning Goal
Use third conditionals and **wish + past perfect** for unreal past situations.

## Core Grammar
Use **if + had + past participle, would have + past participle** for an unreal past result. Use **wish + had + past participle** for regret about the past.

## Examples
- If I had checked the tense, I would have answered correctly.
- I wish I had reviewed Passive 1 before Passive 2.
- If I had known the rule, I wouldn't have chosen that answer.

## Jason's Confusing Points
- **Had known** is not normal past perfect here only; it marks an unreal past condition.
- **Would have** belongs in the result clause, not the if-clause.

## Wrong Answers
- Wrong: If I would have known, I would have answered.
- Correct: If I had known, I would have answered.
- Wrong: I wish I knew it yesterday.
- Better: I wish I had known it yesterday.

## One-line Summary
Use **if/wish + had done** for unreal or regretted past situations.
